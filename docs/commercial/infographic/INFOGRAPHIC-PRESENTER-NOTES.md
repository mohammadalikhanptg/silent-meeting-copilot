# Infographic Presenter Notes

A three-to-five minute spoken walkthrough for showing the Silent Meeting Copilot commercial infographic. Plain language, no jargon. Every claim here matches the truth brief. Where the name comes up, say "working name" once and move on.

## 1. Opening (about 20 seconds)

"This is a private, real-time meeting assistant. The name on it is a working name; the final brand is going through trademark clearance. What it actually does is coach you while a meeting is happening, and then hand you the record afterwards. It is not another note-taker."

## 2. The problem (about 30 seconds)

"Every meeting tool on the market helps you after the meeting. They summarise, they take notes. None of them help you in the moment. While you are concentrating on the conversation, you lose track of the questions the other side raised, you drift off your own objective, and in an interview you cannot tell which of a candidate's claims are actually backed up. By the time you see the notes, it is too late to do anything about it."

## 3. The product (about 25 seconds)

"This works during the conversation. It captures your side and the other side as two separate channels, transcribes them live, and coaches you as you speak. Point to the cockpit image. That is a representation, not a live screen. On the left is what you said, in the middle what they said, and on the right the live coaching: a suggested line with the key phrase highlighted, how well you are tracking your objective, and your talk balance."

## 4. How it works (about 30 seconds)

"Four steps, left to right. Capture both sides. Understand them, transcribe and clean the text in near real time, in English or in Hindi and Urdu. Coach you live with suggestions, drift alerts and open items. And at the end, give you the evidence and the outcome: minutes, transcript, action points, and for interviews a cited evidence pack. Everything in green is available today. The online meeting bot underneath is still in development."

## 5. Capabilities (about 40 seconds)

"Six things it does. Live coaching. Capture and transcription of both sides. An interviewer assistant that prompts you on claims to verify and then produces a defensible evidence pack. Meeting records: minutes, transcript, action points. Privacy controls. And, in development, an optional bot that can join an online call. Five of these are live now; the bot is the one still being proven."

## 6. Value (about 30 seconds)

"What you get out of it: you say the right thing in the moment instead of thinking of it afterwards. You never lose an open question or your objective. Your conversations are more balanced. For hiring, you get consistent, evidence-based candidate reviews you can actually defend. You get a meeting record without taking notes. And you get privacy controls you can show a customer."

## 7. Trust (about 30 seconds)

"This matters for a tool that listens to conversations. Audio recording is off by default and only turns on with consent for that session. There are retention limits and a permanent delete. Your content is opted out of training third-party models. Sign-in is secured with a magic link plus an app code. And the interview review is deliberately evidence-based and disclaimered, it is not a machine deciding who to hire, and the coach never makes things up."

## 8. Current status (about 30 seconds)

"I am being honest about where it is. The green column is live now: capture, transcription, coaching, the interviewer assistant, records, privacy, sign-in, and the desktop helper on Windows and Mac. The amber column is in development: the commercial polish, the meeting bot, optional audio retention, the signed installer, and the access hardening we do before inviting outside users. The blue column is planned next: billing, plans, insights, onboarding and a public website."

## 9. Closing (about 20 seconds)

"So, in one line: help in the moment, and a record you can trust. It is in active build. The launch scope is the base Meeting Coach plus the Interviewer add-on. It is not sold yet, and I am not quoting a price today because the packaging is still being finalised."

## Handling likely questions

- "Is this unique?" No, and I would not claim that. Live coaching is now a competitive space. What stands out is the interviewer assistant with a cited evidence pack, which does not have a direct equivalent among the mainstream note-takers.
- "Can I use it on Zoom or Teams today?" Today it works from your own audio through the desktop helper, so it is not tied to one platform. The bot that joins an online meeting directly is in development.
- "What does it cost?" Not set yet. Pricing and packaging are being finalised.
- "Can my team use it?" Single operator today. Multi-user access is being hardened before we invite external users.
