# FABLE WEBSITE, POSITIONING AND GO-TO-MARKET PLAN
Silent Meeting Copilot (SMC). Independent Fable 5 review, 6 July 2026.
All copy below uses BRAND as a placeholder for the post-rename name. Nothing public ships before the rename clears (hard sequence rule).

## 1. Positioning statement

For recruiters and hiring teams who interview over video, BRAND is a private interview copilot that coaches the interviewer live and verifies what candidates claim, producing a cited evidence pack for every interview. Unlike note-takers and post-call analytics, BRAND works for your side of the conversation while it is happening, sends no bot into the call, and keeps the hiring decision with the human.

Positioning rules:
- Never claim to be the only live coach (falsified; see master report).
- Never position toward candidates or anything that smells like interview cheating; the ethics boundary is a brand asset, state it openly.
- Lead with the interviewer/recruiter story; the general Meeting Coach is the platform tier, presented second.

## 2. Website structure (recommended)

Phase W1 (with rename, pre-full-launch): single landing page plus privacy, terms, security pages and a demo-request form. That is four pages. Do not build more until design partners exist.

Phase W2 (full site, in the 61 to 90 day window):
- Home
- Product: Interview Copilot (flagship)
- Product: Meeting Coach (platform/base)
- Security and Privacy (trust surface)
- Pricing
- About / Why we built this (ethics story, PTG)
- Resources: consent guidance, FAQ, later case studies
- Demo request / Sign in

Primary headline options:
1. "Know who you're really hiring, while you're still on the call."
2. "Live interview coaching. Cited candidate verification. Your call, your side."
3. "The interview copilot for the interviewer."
4. "Interview with evidence, not gut feel."

Subheadline options:
1. "BRAND listens on your side of the interview, prompts the questions worth asking, flags claims worth checking, and hands you a cited evidence pack when it ends."
2. "Private, real-time coaching for recruiters. No bot in the meeting. No decisions made for you."
3. "Candidates are using AI in interviews. Now interviewers have something better: evidence."

Above-the-fold: headline, subheadline, demo-request CTA, and a product shot of the live cockpit with the Live Focus card plus a claims-to-verify moment. Secondary CTA: "See a sample evidence pack" (gated download, doubles as lead capture).

## 4. Narrative sections (in order)

1. Problem: interviews are the weakest link in hiring. Embellished CVs, rehearsed answers, AI-assisted candidates, and decisions defended by memory. (Persona-true, urgent, no competitor mention needed.)
2. Solution walkthrough: before (paste the JD and CV, set the objective), during (live prompts: claims to verify, cross-questions, competency gaps, stay-on-track), after (cited assessment, three-state signal, evidence table, downloadable pack).
3. How it is different: works on your side only; no bot joins the call; works on Zoom, Teams, Meet or phone; every claim in the assessment carries a citation to the transcript; the decision stays human.
4. Ethics section (distinctive): "What BRAND will never do." No answer-feeding, no candidate impersonation, no automated verdicts. This section converts sceptics and pre-empts the fairness objection.
5. Meeting Coach platform section: the same engine coaches any meeting: objectives, drift alerts, open items, talk balance, minutes and action points. Positions the base tier and future add-ons.
6. Multilingual: English plus Hindi/Urdu code-mix live transcription. Short section; large signal to the right buyers.
7. Trust signals: security page teaser (session-bound tokens, strict CSP, retention and hard delete, consent acknowledgement per session, AI-provider opt-out), GDPR/DPA availability, sub-processor transparency. After counsel review only.
8. Social proof: founding-agency logos and one pull-quote per partner as they land; until then, "Founding agency programme now open" with limited-seats framing.
9. FAQ themes: consent and legality of recording; candidate transparency; data retention and deletion; what the AI can and cannot decide; platform compatibility; pricing basis; what happens if the connection drops; who can see my data.

## 5. Pricing page recommendation

- Show seat-based tiers with included usage; per-interview add-on economics explained in plain terms; enterprise as "talk to us."
- Publish prices once design-partner validation is done; before that, the pricing page says "Founding programme" with a form. Hiding prices forever is a trust cost; hiding them for 90 days while learning is fine.
- Include a fair-use explanation of processed hours in one paragraph, because metering ambiguity is a churn generator.

## 6. Demo and lead capture flow

1. Demo request form (name, agency, seats, interview volume per month, platform used). Volume and platform questions qualify the lead and feed pricing research.
2. Auto-reply with the sample evidence pack PDF.
3. 15-minute live demo on the canned staged interview; end on the generated pack.
4. Founding-programme offer with a one-page terms sheet (price, term, feedback commitment, case-study rights).
5. Onboarding call: helper install, pairing, consent flow, first real interview scheduled.

Assets this flow needs created: sample evidence pack (sanitised), canned demo interview script plus reference CV, one-page founding terms sheet, install guide, consent guidance one-pager, security page, three-minute product video (screen capture is sufficient).

## 7. Launch sequence (sequenced against the rename)

1. Name cleared, domains registered, brand token flipped. Nothing public before this.
2. W1 landing page plus trust pages live. Demo pipeline opens.
3. Private founding programme (no public announcement): direct outreach only. Product hardens against real users.
4. Public soft launch: full W2 site, published pricing, first case study, LinkedIn announcement from Mo and PTG, listings in recruitment-tech directories. External research required: which UK recruitment industry directories, newsletters and communities carry weight (for example UK recruiter LinkedIn groups, industry press); compile during the design-partner phase.
5. Post-launch: bot convenience feature announcement when proven ("BRAND can now join the call for you"), then insights.

## 8. First ten sales conversations plan

- Source: Mo's own network first (PTG clients, IT/recruitment adjacency), then warm LinkedIn outreach to UK agency owners; ten booked conversations is the target, not ten closes.
- Script skeleton: 5 minutes on their interview process and pain (record verbatim phrases for copy), 10 minutes demo, 5 minutes founding offer and price-anchor test (GBP 29/49/79 ladder), close on a yes/no/why.
- Instrument every call: objection log, exact words used for the problem, reaction to pricing anchors, platform mix (Zoom versus Teams matters enormously and validates the helper-first decision).
- Success criteria: 3 or more founding sign-ups from 10 conversations validates the wedge; 0 to 1 means revisit ICP or price before building Stripe.

## 9. First 90 days GTM plan (aligned to master report 30/60/90)

Days 0 to 30: naming and legal clocks started; landing page drafted (unpublished); demo assets built; outreach list of 30 target agencies compiled; zero public activity.
Days 31 to 60: rename flips; W1 pages live; ten conversations executed; 3 to 5 founding partners onboarded; copy iterated from verbatim buyer language.
Days 61 to 90: pricing finalised from evidence; W2 site and pricing page live; first case study; public soft launch; begin content cadence (one practical piece per fortnight: interview-integrity checklists, consent guides; these double as SEO assets in an uncontested keyword space, external research required to confirm keyword landscape).

## 10. Messaging for the base Meeting Coach (secondary persona)

Keep a single page, not a campaign: "The same engine, for every meeting that matters." Target: professionals running high-stakes client calls. Do not spend acquisition effort here in the first 90 days; it exists so the platform story is visible to investors and so interviewer customers discover the base tier organically.
