# Response Bank (example pack)

Prepared lines shown by the manual buttons. No API needed.
Format: a section header "## <button_key>" followed by the lines to display.
Keep lines short enough to read mid-meeting. No real case facts here.

## stay_on_agenda
- Let's keep to what we agreed to cover today.
- Can we park that and return to the agenda item in front of us?

## ask_for_written_proposal
- Please put that proposal in writing so we can review it properly.
- I'm not going to respond to that verbally. Send it through and we'll consider it.

## house_property_issue
- Property matters need to go through the correct process, not a verbal exchange.
- I'm not discussing the property point informally. It goes in writing.

## business_ownership_claim
- Ownership is a matter of record. Let's rely on the documents, not memory.
- I won't accept or deny that verbally. It needs evidence.

## financial_claim
- I'm not agreeing any number here. Put the figures and basis in writing.
- Send the breakdown and we'll review it. No verbal commitment today.

## allegation_response
- I don't accept that characterisation. Let's stick to specifics and facts.
- If there's a specific point, put it in writing with detail.

## shouting_boundary
- I'm happy to continue when the tone is calm. Let's take a short pause.
- Raising voices won't move this forward. Let's keep it civil or reconvene.

## staff_partner_contradiction
- That contradicts what was said earlier. Which position are we working from?
- Let's get one consistent position in writing before we go further.

## legal_solicitor_route
- This may be better handled through our advisers. I'll take it to mine.
- I'm not resolving a legal point in this room. It goes to the solicitors.
